Startups and product teams live or die by how quickly they learn.

The sooner you know whether an idea works, the sooner you can double down or move on.

**Why most ideas don't need perfect code:**

A hypothesis is a belief you haven't proven yet:
- "Users will pay for X"
- "This workflow will reduce churn"
- "This feature will unlock enterprise deals"

You don't prove those with perfect code. You prove them with **evidence**: real people using something, real feedback, real behavior.

**Perfect code is for when you already know you're building the right thing.**

For validation, you need something that works well enough to generate a signal.

**What "fail fast" really means:**

Design your process so you discover failure (or success) as early and cheaply as possible.

- Run a small experiment
- Get a clear result
- If wrong: stop before investing a fortune
- If right: learn in weeks (not quarters)

**The cost of failure is low. The speed of learning is high.**
